Popular Types of Reclaimed Wood

We often see different types of reclaimed wood. Each has its own look and benefits. The type of wood used in reclaimed wood cabinetry can change how a room feels.

Barn Wood

Barn wood is a favorite for rustic kitchen cabinets. It looks weathered and worn. This wood comes from old barns, adding a nostalgic feel to homes.

Pallet Wood

Pallet wood is cheaper and used for eco-friendly cabinetry. It's light and has a smooth texture, making it easy to work with.

Old Growth Timber

Old growth timber is loved for its strength and beauty. It comes from ancient trees. This wood has a special look that's hard to find in new wood.

Maintenance and Care for Reclaimed Wood Cabinetry

We know how important it is to take care of your reclaimed wood cabinetry. It should last for many years. Clean it with a soft cloth and mild soap to keep it looking great. For rustic kitchen cabinets, avoid spills, scratches, and other damage.

To keep your eco-friendly cabinetry in top shape, follow these tips:

  • Regularly dust the surfaces with a soft, dry cloth to prevent dirt buildup.
  • Use coasters, placemats, and trivets to reduce the occurrence of heat marks and scratches.
  • Perform seasonal maintenance checks, including tightening loose screws or joints to maintain stability.

For deeper cleaning, use a pH-neutral cleaner made for wood. Stay away from harsh chemicals like bleach and vinegar. Remember, reclaimed wood cabinetry needs basic care to keep its look and last long.

By following these easy care tips, you can enjoy your reclaimed wood cabinetry for a long time. You'll also help the environment with your eco-friendly cabinetry choice.

Cost Factors to Consider

When you add reclaimed wood cabinetry to your home, think about the cost. The price can change a lot. It depends on the wood type, design, and how it's installed. We look at the value of using reclaimed wood for its lasting quality, green benefits, and beauty.

Some important things to think about when planning your budget include:

  • Material costs: Reclaimed timber cupboards can cost between $500 and $1,200 per foot. This depends on the wood quality.
  • Design complexity: Fancy or unique designs can make the price go up by 5-15%.
  • Installation method: Getting a pro to install can add 15-30% to the cost. Doing it yourself might save money but could lead to errors.

Upcycled wood storage can also be a good deal. Prices are from $100 to $500 per unit, based on the design. Think about whether to do it yourself or hire a pro. With careful planning, you can make a beautiful space that fits your budget.
