Maintenance Tips for Indoor Outdoor Kitchens

Keeping your indoor outdoor kitchen looking good and working well is key. Regular cleaning and getting ready for the seasons are musts. We suggest a plan for daily care and for when the weather changes.

Routine Cleaning and Care

For stainless steel parts, use a soft cleaner and follow the grain to avoid rust. Powder-coated stainless steel is easier to keep up than bare steel. Concrete and wood need extra care to fight moisture and prevent damage.

Quartzite and soapstone countertops should be wiped down often to avoid stains. Clean tile surfaces with a tile cleaner.

Seasonal Preparations

In cold areas, winterize your outdoor kitchen by draining water lines and covering cooking gear to stop freeze damage. In wet places, keep good air flow to stop mold and mildew. By taking care of seasonal needs, your kitchen will stay great all year, bringing joy and value to your home.
