Size Considerations for Your Kitchen Island

When you design your kitchen, the size of your kitchen island is key. A well-sized island makes your kitchen better, not crowded. We'll show you how to measure your space, pick the right size, and why space for walking and access is important.

Measuring Your Space

Start by measuring your kitchen space carefully. It's important to have enough room to move around easily. An island should only take up to 10% of your total kitchen space.

Ideal Dimensions for Different Layouts

The best size for your island depends on your kitchen's layout. A usual island is 24 to 36 inches deep and 36 inches high. Add 42 inches in height for bar seating. Give each seat 24 inches of space and 18 to 24 inches in depth. You should have 36 to 42 inches of walking space around the island. This makes your kitchen work well, even with more than one person cooking or if someone needs more room to move.

Traffic Flow and Accessibility

Good movement and access around your island are key. Leave 42 to 48 inches of space for easy walking around it. If your island has seats, keep 44 inches of walking space behind them. This way, your kitchen stays easy to move in and use, no matter what you're doing.

Feature Standard Dimension
Overall Size 4 feet by 2 feet minimum
Depth 24 to 36 inches (up to 42 inches for appliances/sinks)
Height 36 inches (42 inches for bar seating)
Walking Space Around 42 to 48 inches
Seating Overhang 12 to 18 inches
Clear Space for Sinks 18 inches on one side minimum
Clear Space for Cooktops 15 inches on one side; 12 inches on the other
